02 — Discovery

Problem &
Solution

02
Key Discovery

We expected scheduling friction to be the #1 problem. What research actually showed: 7 of 12 participants abandoned at doctor selection — not at payment or confirmation. The real friction wasn't the form. It was the trust gap at the moment of choice.

01
User Problem

Filters exist. Nobody uses them.

We built our first prototype with 9 filter chips across specialty, location, language, and rating. In testing, 9 of 12 participants typed directly into search and never touched a single filter. The problem wasn't missing filters — it was that filters weren't where users looked first.

Users scanned for a name or specialty keyword before anything else. Search-first behaviour was universal across all age groups tested.
01
The Solution

Structured filtering with live availability

A smart search layer with specialty, location, language, and rating filters — combined with real-time slot visibility. Users see exactly when a doctor is free before tapping into their profile.

02
User Problem

Users don't compare doctors. They pick the first "safe enough" one.

We assumed users would weigh credentials and reviews. What we observed: most participants spent under 12 seconds on a profile before deciding. The #1 trust signal wasn't qualifications or star rating — it was response time. "Usually replies in 2 hours" converted better than "15 years experience."

P07 (Interview): "I don't need to know their whole CV. I just need to feel like they'll actually respond to me."
02
The Solution

Rich doctor profiles that build trust

Detailed profiles surface verified credentials, patient reviews, video introductions, and specialty highlights. Trust scores and badge systems let users make confident decisions faster.

03
User Problem

Drop-off wasn't where we expected it.

We assumed users abandoned at payment — that's where every funnel analysis points. But drop-off spiked earlier: at slot selection, specifically when no same-week availability was visible. Users didn't assume the doctor was busy. They assumed the app was broken, and left.

63% of abandoned sessions in the analytics audit ended on the slot selection screen — not the payment screen.
03
The Solution

3-step booking with clear progress states

A streamlined booking flow: Pick slot → Confirm details → Done. Progress indicators, animated confirmations, and instant calendar sync make the experience feel fast and certain.

04
User Problem

Users didn't ask for "messaging." They asked for a quick question.

Early on, we scoped a full in-app messaging platform. But what participants actually said in interviews was more specific: "I just want to send one question without booking another appointment." The need wasn't a chat product — it was low-friction access to their doctor for small, anxious moments.

8 of 12 interview participants had texted or WhatsApp'd their doctor outside the app within the past month. None felt good about it.
04
The Solution

In-app messaging and visit history

A post-visit hub with secure doctor messaging, visit summaries, prescription history, and smart follow-up reminders — keeping the care relationship alive long after the appointment ends.
