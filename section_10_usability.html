Critical
Filter chips were invisible on load
5 of 6 users scrolled straight past the filter chips and dove into doctor cards, missing specialty and location filters entirely. The chips sat flush to the search bar with no visual separation or label to indicate they were interactive.
5 of 6 users affected
Fixed: Added a labelled "Filter by" header above the chips with clear visual separation from the search bar.
Critical
Date and time selection caused drop-offs
4 users tapped a date and immediately tried to confirm — not realising they still needed to pick a time slot below. The calendar and slot grid were visually separate with no indication of a two-step sequence.
4 of 6 users affected
Fixed: Added a 2-step indicator "1 · Select Date → 2 · Pick Time" above the calendar so users always know where they are in the flow.
Critical
Doctor profiles didn't build enough trust
4 users spent over 3 minutes on a doctor's profile but still left without booking. They couldn't quickly verify credentials, see how many people had rated the doctor, or confirm the doctor was legitimate — leading to hesitation and abandonment.
4 of 6 users affected
Fixed: Added a verified badge, rating with review count, years of experience, and hospital affiliation — all visible above the fold on the doctor profile.
Moderate
Post-booking screen felt like a dead end
3 users who completed a booking didn't know what to do next. Several tapped the back button to check if the appointment had actually saved. There was no indication of next steps or what to expect before the visit.
3 of 6 users affected
Fixed: Confirmation screen now shows a clear success state with appointment details, a pre-visit checklist teaser, and a prompt to view the upcoming appointment.
Moderate
Home screen didn't adapt after booking
3 users who returned to the home screen after booking were confused to see the same empty state. There was no sign their appointment existed unless they navigated into the Appointments tab — the home screen had no awareness of their context.
3 of 6 users affected
Fixed: Home now surfaces the upcoming appointment as a contextual card with doctor info, date, time, and a quick-action pre-visit checklist strip.
Moderate
No way to prepare or reach the doctor before a visit
2 users felt anxious in the days before their appointment — unsure what to bring, how to prepare, or if they could ask the doctor a quick question without calling the clinic. The app offered no pre-visit touchpoint.
2 of 6 users affected
Fixed: Added a pre-visit checklist with doctor-specific notes and a direct message CTA — giving users a way to prepare and communicate before arriving.
What Users Said vs. What They Did
What they said
"The app felt pretty straightforward" — 4 of 6 participants rated the overall experience 4 or 5 out of 5 on the post-session survey. Most said they'd use it over calling a clinic.
⟷
What they did
5 of 6 never touched the filter chips. 4 of 6 got stuck on slot selection. 3 of 6 went back to check if their booking was actually saved. Average task completion in Round 1 was 60%.
This gap — between how users felt about the experience and how they actually performed — is why watching session recordings matters more than reading survey scores. We would have shipped a broken flow if we'd relied on the rating alone.
Unexpected Finding

We expected payment to be the highest-anxiety moment. It wasn't. The biggest pause in every session happened when users returned to the home screen after booking and saw no trace of their appointment. Several said nothing — they just stared at the screen for 4–6 seconds, then tapped back. One participant said "did it even go through?" and started the flow again from scratch. The empty home state wasn't a visual design problem. It was a trust problem — and we only caught it because we watched the recordings, not just the task success metrics.

Key Change
Home screen — before & after testing
Before testing
4-icon horizontal row identical in structure to the bottom tab bar — double navigation signal
"Find Doctor" in icon row duplicates the Search tab — users tapped either interchangeably
4 of 6 users paused at the icon row unsure if it was a second nav or clickable shortcuts
After testing
Wide cards with height, color, icon + subtitle read as content shortcuts — not a second nav bar
Pre-visit checklist strip inside appointment card adds contextual depth without adding another section
Navigation confusion dropped to 0 in round 2 — all users understood the bottom bar as the only nav
Key Change
Slot selection — before & after testing
Before testing
No step indicator — users didn't know they had to pick both a date AND a time
Slot grid appeared immediately with no visual relationship to the calendar above
4 of 6 users tapped Confirm without selecting a time slot
After testing
2-step indicator makes date → time sequence explicit and scannable
"Available times" label with a green divider visually connects the slot grid to the selected date
Task completion rate for slot selection improved from 33% to 100% in round 2
